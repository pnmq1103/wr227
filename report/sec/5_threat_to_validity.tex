\section{Threats To Validity}
In this study, several threats to validity  must be acknowledged.

A primary assumption of our Phase 1 pretraining is that empirical Fisher Information serves as an accurate proxy for ground-truth visual saliency. Gradients from a cross-entropy loss may not fully capture complex, multi-token spatial dependencies. Furthermore, token pruning can result in a worse accuracy, especially with the structure of chunkwise linear attention.

The inherent variance of Proximal Policy Optimization poses an internal threat. RL algorithms are notoriously sensitive to hyperparameter configurations, specifically the sparsity penalty coefficient ($\lambda$) and the entropy bonus.

Current evaluation is primarily constrained to image classification tasks. It remains to be empirically validated how well this localized, independent agent formulation generalizes to dense prediction tasks, such as high-resolution object detection or semantic segmentation, where maintaining precise background context is often more critical than in image-level classification.
