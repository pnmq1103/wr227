\subsection{Datasets}

To evaluate the effectiveness of our proposed framework, we conduct experiments on two standard image classification datasets: CIFAR-10 and ImageNet-100. The selection of these specific datasets is driven by a balance between computational feasibility and representative complexity. Training on these subsets significantly reduces the training time per epoch, allowing for rapid iteration and prototyping on accessible consumer-grade hardware.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
We evaluate the proposed framework against several state-of-the-art models, including architectures based on standard softmax and linear attention mechanisms. Furthermore, we compare our approach against alternative gating methods, such as the sigmoid function. In the current work, our evaluation focuses strictly on the image classification task, measuring Top-1 accuracy. Extending this framework to other dense vision tasks, such as object detection and semantic segmentation, is left for future work.
\subsection{Implementation Details}
All models are implemented and trained utilizing a combination of a local NVIDIA RTX 5060 Ti and Kaggle Tesla T4 GPU accelerators. Prior to feature extraction, all input images are resized to a fixed spatial resolution of $144 \times 144$. For the MARL reward formulation, the computational budget penalty coefficient ($\lambda_b$) is set to $0.05$, and the sparsity penalty coefficient ($\lambda_s$) is set to $1.0$. The entire training process is conducted over $40$ epochs.

In our comparative analysis, we include RMT \cite{fan2024rmt} and RALVT \cite{fan2025breaking} as representative state-of-the-art vision architectures. To specifically isolate the impact of our proposed gating mechanism, we define multiple baseline variants built upon the RALVT framework. The \textbf{Vanilla Baseline} consists of a standard linear attention model. The \textbf{Baseline + Softmax} variant incorporates the softmax ranking strategy introduced by RALA \cite{fan2025breaking}, while the \textbf{Baseline + Sigmoid} utilizes a simple sigmoid function for token gating. Finally, \textbf{Baseline + MARL} represents our proposed methodology, replacing standard routing methods with our decentralized reinforcement learning gating framework. Our evaluation reveals that the proposed token gating mechanism significantly outperforms the softmax-based weighting strategy adopted from RALA. However, the ungated vanilla baseline still achieves higher accuracy than the gated variants. We hypothesize that this discrepancy stems from the restricted image resolution ($144 \times 144$) utilized in our current experiments; at such a small scale, explicitly weighting or gating tokens may inadvertently discard critical spatial features, thereby hindering overall performance. Furthermore, while the RMT and RALVT baselines achieve substantially superior results, this performance gap is primarily attributable to their highly optimized architectures and extensively tuned training recipes.

\subsection{Qualitative Study}
\paragraph{Gating Map Visualization}

To qualitatively evaluate the token selection behavior of our proposed framework against the baselines, we visualize the spatial routing maps generated at deeper layers of the network. 
Observing the \textbf{RALA (Softmax)} map (Figure~\ref{fig:gating_rala}), we see clear visual evidence of the softmax saturation problem discussed earlier; the global normalization across the sequence heavily dilutes the token weights, resulting in a dark, near-zero activation map that chokes information flow. In contrast, the \textbf{Sigmoid} (Figure~\ref{fig:gating_map_sigmoid}) baseline produces an aggressive, high-contrast saliency map that heavily activates on the primary subject while completely zeroing out the background. Our proposed \textbf{MARL} (Figure~\ref{fig:gating_map_marl}) framework produces a softer, continuous gating distribution. We attribute this less decisive gating behavior to current limitations in hyperparameter optimization, suggesting that the sparsity penalty coefficients require further tuning to enforce strict bimodal survival weights.
